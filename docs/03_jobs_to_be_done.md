## Document Information

**Project:** VisitBrief AI
**Document:** Jobs To Be Done
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Discovery Phase

---

# Purpose

This document identifies the underlying jobs users are trying to accomplish when preparing for a home visit.

The focus is on outcomes rather than features.

Users do not hire products because they want technology. They hire products because they want to make progress toward a goal.

---

# Primary Job

## Functional Job

When preparing for a home visit,

I want to quickly understand the most important information in the case,

so that I can arrive informed and prepared.

---

# Supporting Jobs

## Job 1 — Understand Case Background

When I receive a case assignment,

I want to quickly understand the history of the case,

so that I know what context is relevant before the visit.

---

## Job 2 — Understand Recent Activity

When preparing for a visit,

I want to identify recent updates,

so that I understand what has changed since previous interactions.

---

## Job 3 — Identify Open Follow-Ups

When reviewing case records,

I want to know which items still require attention,

so that I do not overlook important follow-up activities.

---

## Job 4 — Identify Deadlines

When preparing for a visit,

I want to know what deadlines are approaching,

so that required actions are completed on time.

---

## Job 5 — Identify Missing Information

When reviewing records,

I want to know what information is incomplete or unverified,

so that I can seek clarification when appropriate.

---

## Job 6 — Identify Conflicting Information

When reviewing documentation,

I want to know whether records contain conflicting information,

so that I can verify facts before acting on them.

---

# Emotional Jobs

## Feel Prepared

When arriving for a visit,

I want confidence that I understand the case,

so that I can focus on the interaction rather than searching for information.

---

## Reduce Administrative Stress

When reviewing documentation,

I want less time spent searching through records,

so that I can spend more time on meaningful work.

---

# Social Jobs

## Demonstrate Professional Preparedness

When discussing a case with supervisors or colleagues,

I want to demonstrate that I understand the case history,

so that decisions are informed by accurate information.

---

# Jobs Outside Product Scope

The following jobs are intentionally not supported by VisitBrief AI:

* Determining risk levels
* Making safety decisions
* Recommending placements
* Recommending interventions
* Determining eligibility
* Making legal decisions
* Approving service plans

These jobs remain the responsibility of qualified professionals.

---

# Product Design Implications

The JTBD analysis suggests that users are primarily trying to:

1. Understand relevant information quickly.
2. Identify what requires attention.
3. Prepare effectively before a visit.

The product should therefore focus on:

* Information retrieval
* Summarization
* Source traceability
* Missing-information identification
* Conflict detection

The product should not focus on decision automation.
