# Purpose

This document defines the launch strategy for VisitBrief AI.

The objective is to introduce a responsible AI-assisted visit preparation workflow that helps caseworkers prepare for home visits more efficiently while maintaining transparency, traceability, and human accountability.

The launch focuses on improving preparation workflows without introducing decision automation.

---

# Product Vision

VisitBrief AI helps caseworkers prepare for home visits by transforming approved case records into grounded, cited, visit-focused briefings.

The system supports information retrieval and preparation while preserving human ownership of professional decisions.

---

# Launch Objectives

The launch should demonstrate that VisitBrief AI can:

- Improve visit-preparation efficiency
- Improve access to relevant case information
- Surface missing information
- Surface conflicting information
- Provide source traceability
- Support responsible AI usage
- Maintain human accountability

# Launch Scope

The initial launch includes:

- Synthetic case library
- Case selection
- Visit brief generation
- Case-specific question answering
- Source citations
- Missing-information flags
- Conflict detection
- Refusal behavior
- Human-in-the-loop safeguards

---

# Launch Exclusions

The initial launch excludes:

- Risk scoring
- Placement recommendations
- Safety recommendations
- Eligibility determinations
- Legal guidance
- Predictive models
- Automated decision-making
- Real agency integrations
- Real client data

---

# User Training Requirements

Users should understand:

- The system supports preparation only.
- The system does not make decisions.
- Responses should be verified using citations.
- Missing information should be reviewed.
- Conflicting information should be verified.
- Professional judgment remains with the user.

---

# Responsible AI Communication

Users should be informed that:

- The assistant retrieves information from approved records.
- Responses are grounded in available evidence.
- Information may be incomplete.
- Missing or conflicting information may require additional review.
- The assistant does not replace professional judgment.

# Launch Decision

The system should prioritize transparency, traceability, and human accountability over automation.

Success is defined by improved preparation efficiency while maintaining responsible AI guardrails and professional oversight.
