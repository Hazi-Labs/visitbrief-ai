# VisitBrief AI — User Workflow

## Document Information

**Project:** VisitBrief AI
**Document:** User Workflow Map
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Discovery Phase

---

# Purpose

This document describes the current and future workflow for a caseworker preparing for a home visit.

The objective is to understand where effort is currently spent, where friction exists, and where AI may safely provide assistance.

The workflow described here is a hypothetical portfolio scenario using synthetic data.

---

# Primary User

Caseworker preparing for an assigned home visit.

Primary goal:

> Understand the case, prepare for the visit, and identify any follow-up items before meeting with the family.

---

# Current-State Workflow (Without VisitBrief AI)

## Step 1 — Receive Assignment

The caseworker receives notification that a home visit must be completed.

Typical questions:

* Which case is assigned?
* When is the visit due?
* What should I review before the visit?

---

## Step 2 — Locate Records

The worker opens the case record and gathers relevant documentation.

Examples:

* Intake summary
* Contact notes
* Previous visit notes
* Service plan updates
* Provider communications
* Administrative tasks
* Deadlines

Potential friction:

* Information spread across multiple records
* Multiple documents require review

---

## Step 3 — Review Case History

The worker manually reviews records.

Typical questions:

* What happened previously?
* What concerns were documented?
* What follow-up items remain open?
* What changed recently?

Potential friction:

* Large volume of documentation
* Important details buried in older notes

---

## Step 4 — Identify Missing Information

The worker attempts to determine whether information is incomplete.

Examples:

* Missing documents
* Missing contact information
* Unverified details
* Outstanding questions

Potential friction:

* Missing information may not be obvious
* Requires careful review across records

---

## Step 5 — Identify Conflicting Information

The worker attempts to determine whether records disagree.

Examples:

* Different contact information
* Conflicting dates
* Different household descriptions

Potential friction:

* Conflicts may appear across multiple notes
* Manual comparison required

---

## Step 6 — Prepare for Visit

The worker organizes information mentally or through personal notes.

Typical questions:

* What should I follow up on?
* What information needs clarification?
* What happened since the last visit?

Potential friction:

* Time pressure
* Repetitive preparation effort

---

## Step 7 — Conduct Home Visit

The worker meets with the family.

The worker applies professional judgment and follows organizational procedures.

Important:

Professional judgment occurs here.

This activity should remain human-led.

---

## Step 8 — Post-Visit Documentation

The worker documents observations, updates records, and completes follow-up tasks.

---

# Future-State Workflow (With VisitBrief AI)

## Step 1 — Select Assigned Case

The worker selects a case inside VisitBrief AI.

The system loads only the selected synthetic case records.

---

## Step 2 — Generate Visit Brief

The worker clicks:

"Generate Visit Brief"

The system produces:

* Case background
* Recent updates
* Relevant concerns
* Upcoming tasks
* Deadlines
* Missing information
* Conflicting information
* Source citations

---

## Step 3 — Review Generated Brief

The worker reviews the generated summary.

The worker may navigate to original records if additional context is required.

---

## Step 4 — Ask Case-Specific Questions

Examples:

* What was the most recent contact note?
* What deadlines are upcoming?
* What concerns were documented?
* Which information appears missing?

The assistant provides factual answers with citations.

---

## Step 5 — Review Source Records

The worker reviews source records when:

* Information is incomplete
* Information is conflicting
* Decisions must be made
* Additional context is required

---

## Step 6 — Prepare for Visit

The worker uses the summarized information to prepare for the visit.

Professional judgment remains with the worker.

---

## Step 7 — Conduct Visit

The worker conducts the visit.

The AI assistant does not participate in decision-making.

---

# AI Assistance Opportunities

The workflow suggests AI may help with:

* Summarization
* Information retrieval
* Source traceability
* Missing-information detection
* Conflict detection
* Natural-language question answering

---

# Human-Owned Activities

The workflow suggests these activities should remain human-owned:

* Case decisions
* Safety determinations
* Placement decisions
* Eligibility decisions
* Legal decisions
* Service-plan decisions
* Supervisory review

---

# Workflow Design Principle

VisitBrief AI should reduce the effort required to locate and organize information while preserving human ownership of all professional decisions.

The system is designed to support preparation, not decision-making.
